    01-Optional Rules - Distress Points (DSP)

    01-Optional Rules - Distress Points (DSP)

    Optional Expanded Sanity Rules for Call of Cthulhu

    Note: These rules are optional and will be discussed during Session Zero. They are designed to enhance the horror experience in long-term campaigns without incapacitating characters through excessive Sanity loss.

    These rules were heavily inspired by the work Apostol Apostolov at  Chaosium Call of Cthulhu 7th Edition House Rules

    For details on making characters see 01-Characters-Caroline

    Introduction

    The Expanded Sanity Rules introduce a new mechanic called Distress Points (DSP) to add psychological depth and realism to our game. Like standard Sanity Points (SAN), which decrease as characters encounter supernatural horrors, Distress Points start at a maximum value and decrease as characters face stress from realistic, non-supernatural threats. This allows characters to handle man-made horrors and stressful situations without immediate risk of madness.

    Distress Points (DSP)

    What Are Distress Points?

    • Distress Points represent your character's mental resilience to stress from non-supernatural situations, such as tough decisions, resource scarcity, or dangerous environments.
    • Every character starts with DSP equal to their POW (similar to how SAN is calculated).

    Losing Distress Points

    • Non-Supernatural Encounters: Non-supernatural horrors cause DSP loss instead of SAN loss. This would replace the traditional sanity roll for these mundane encounters.
    • Stressful Situations: When facing non-horrific, but still stress situations, DSP checks may need to be made.
    • SAN Rolls: Making a sanity roll, by definition, is caused by a situation that is stressful. You lose a a DSP every time a sanity roll is made; pass or fail.
    • Voluntary SAN Damage: You can always choose to take SAN damage instead of losing DSP, but not the reverse.

    Effects of Distress Points

    • Above Half DSP (more than half of your maximum DSP): You remain composed; your hidden traits are under control.
    • Equal to or Below Half DSP (half or less of your maximum DSP):
      • Begin to show minor signs of stress linked to your Dark Trait.
      • Each time you lose 5 or more DSP at once, roll against your current DSP. If you roll equal to or less than your DSP, you experience a Breakdown for 1d4 rounds.
    • Equal to or Below One-Fifth DSP (one-fifth or less of your maximum DSP):
      • Your behavior is noticeably affected by your Dark Trait.
      • Breakdowns last longer (1d10 rounds), and the Keeper may suggest actions for your character.
    • At Zero DSP:
      • You suffer a Complete Breakdown.
      • The Keeper may take control of your character until DSP is recovered.

    Maximum DSP

    • DSP cannot exceed your starting value (POW). Rest and recovery can help you regain lost DSP up to this maximum.

    Example of a Breakdown

    Scenario:

    Maria, an investigator with a starting POW of 60, starts with DSP = 60. Her Dark Trait is Paranoia, which she keeps hidden from the rest of the team.

    Events Leading to the Breakdown:

    1. Initial Stress:
      • Maria and her team are exploring an abandoned asylum.
      • She encounters realistic but disturbing graffiti and unsettling noises.
      • Over time, she loses 20 DSP, reducing her DSP to 40.
    2. DSP at Half Maximum:
      • Since her DSP is now at 40 (which is two-thirds of 60), she remains composed.
    3. Further Stress:
      • The team finds a room with detailed accounts of past tortures (non-supernatural).
      • Maria loses 15 DSP due to the distressing content, bringing her DSP down to 25.
    4. DSP Falls to Half or Less:
      • With DSP at 25 (less than half of 60), she begins to show minor signs of her Dark Trait (Paranoia).
      • She starts questioning the intentions of her teammates subtly.
    5. Triggering a Breakdown:
      • Suddenly, a realistic but horrific trap is sprung, causing Maria to lose 10 DSP at once, dropping her DSP to 15.
      • Because she lost 5 or more DSP at once and her DSP is below half, she must roll against her current DSP (15).
        • She rolls a 22, which is above her DSP.
        • Breakdown Triggered: The Breakdown will last 1d4 rounds (let's say she rolls a 3).

    During the Breakdown:

    • Behavior:
      • For the next 3 rounds, Maria's paranoia takes over.
      • She becomes convinced that one of her teammates is sabotaging the mission.
      • She might accuse them openly, refuse to share information, or even act defensively if approached.
    • Keeper's Role:
      • The Keeper reminds Maria's player of her Dark Trait and suggests actions that reflect her heightened paranoia.
      • The player can choose how to role-play these suggestions within the confines of the Breakdown.

    After the Breakdown:

    • Maria's DSP remains at 15.
    • She regains some composure but still shows signs of stress.
    • The team may notice her odd behavior, and she might need to find ways to recover DSP.

    Dark Traits

    What Are Dark Traits?

    • Dark Traits are hidden aspects of your character's personality that emerge under stress.
    • They add depth to your character and provide role-playing opportunities.

    Selecting a Dark Trait

    • Choose a Dark Trait during character creation. It should remain secret from other players but not the keeper.
    • Examples include traits like paranoia, obsession, aggression, or compulsiveness.
    • Your Dark Trait influences how your character behaves as DSP decreases.

    Revealing Dark Traits

    • As DSP decreases, subtle behaviors linked to your Dark Trait begin to surface.
    • Under severe stress or during Breakdowns, these traits may dominate your actions.

    Managing Distress

    • Rest and Relaxation:
      • Short Rest: Resting for at least an hour in a safe, comfortable environment restores 1d10 DSP. This cannot be repeated until at least one point of DSP has been lost.
      • Extended Rest: A full day of rest restores 10 DSP (may be repeated as needed).
    • Composure Attempts:
      • POW Roll: Attempt a POW roll to compose yourself. If your current DSP is at or below half maximum, the roll is of Hard difficulty.
      • Success: Restores 1d4 DSP during danger or 1d6+1 DSP when safe.
    • Assistance from Allies:
      • Another character can use a Psychology roll to help you recover.
      • Success has the same effects as a successful POW roll. These cannot be used in conjugation. But if one fails, the other may be attempted.
    • Indulging in Manias:
      • Engaging in a personal mania restores 1d6+1 DSP.
      • Requires a Sanity roll; failure results in 1 SAN loss.

    Pacing Stress Loss

    • To keep gameplay smooth, DSP losses can be accumulated over a scene and applied at the end.
    • This minimizes interruptions and maintains narrative flow.
    • Immediate DSP losses may still occur during sudden shocks or critical moments.

    Increased Impact of Phobias

    • If your character has a Phobia, encountering its trigger doubles any DSP losses.

    Purpose of These Rules

    • Enhanced Role-Playing: Allows for deeper character development as hidden traits surface under stress.
    • Balanced Horror Experience: Maintains the horror atmosphere without quickly incapacitating characters through massive Sanity loss.
    • Long-Term Campaign Suitability: Ideal for extended play, where character arcs and psychological journeys are explored over time.

    Conclusion

    Please consider how these rules might enhance your gaming experience. They aim to add psychological realism and depth to our campaign, offering new challenges and role-playing opportunities without overwhelming your character with Sanity loss. We'll discuss these rules in detail during Session Zero to ensure everyone is comfortable and understands how they will be integrated into our game.

    List of 50 Example Dark Traits

    The following are example dark traits; others are possible. Please discuss your choice with the keeper before finalizing your selection.

    Dark Traits Table

    • 1. AGGRESSIVE: Physical action and violence are your solutions to every problem.
      • Under Distress: You display bouts of verbal aggression and may intimidate allies who challenge your decisions.
      • Breakdown: You are unlikely to restrain yourself from combat until any perceived enemy is neutralized.
    • 2. MARTYR: You feel overwhelmed by responsibility for others.
      • Under Distress: You become less concerned with your own well-being and focus entirely on your allies' needs.
      • Breakdown: You're willing to sacrifice yourself for a greater goal or the survival of others.
    • 3. COWARD: Fear dominates your actions.
      • Under Distress: You become pessimistic and sarcastic, openly expecting the worst outcomes.
      • Breakdown: You panic and flee, even if it means abandoning your friends in danger.
    • 4. EGOTIST: Your primary concern is your own well-being and survival.
      • Under Distress: Your decisions prioritize your needs over others.
      • Breakdown: You place your safety above everything, even denying help or betraying allies.
    • 5. SADISTIC: You derive pleasure from others' discomfort.
      • Under Distress: You may enjoy causing harm or misfortune to feel better.
      • Breakdown: You pursue actions that cause maximum harm to enemies, even if it jeopardizes plans or allies.
    • 6. PARANOID: You believe others are constantly scheming against you.
      • Under Distress: You see coincidences as evidence of conspiracies.
      • Breakdown: You're convinced immediate events are part of a grand plot and take drastic action to counter it.
    • 7. PSYCHOPATHIC: You disconnect from others' emotions and needs.
      • Under Distress: You focus solely on your goals, unable to communicate intentions or feelings.
      • Breakdown: You lose the ability to cooperate, resolving problems only on your own.
    • 8. ANNOYING: You have habits that irritate others, like excessive talking or fidgeting.
      • Under Distress: These habits intensify, helping you cope with uncertainty.
      • Breakdown: You struggle to concentrate, taking extra time and lacking focus.
    • 9. DEPENDENT: You seem independent but rely on others when stressed.
      • Under Distress: You cling to others for guidance and protection.
      • Breakdown: You're incapable of making decisions without someone telling you what to do.
    • 10. MEGALOMANIAC: You have an insatiable need for power and control.
      • Under Distress: Feeling weak causes you anxiety and frustration.
      • Breakdown: You'll stop at nothing to gain power, even embracing dangerous paths.
    • 11. CURIOUS: Your thirst for knowledge overrides caution.
      • Under Distress: You can't resist uncovering secrets, even risky ones.
      • Breakdown: You're compelled to experiment with newfound knowledge, often recklessly.
    • 12. FIXATED: You focus on repetitive activities to escape stress.
      • Under Distress: You feel compelled to continue these activities.
      • Breakdown: You fixate on one activity as a source of comfort, resisting attempts to stop.
    • 13. LOUD: You express emotions vocally.
      • Under Distress: You can't keep your voice down and openly share your thoughts.
      • Breakdown: You make a scene, yelling or ranting, potentially wasting valuable time.
    • 14. ZEALOUS: A strong belief or moral code anchors you.
      • Under Distress: You obsess over this belief and become anxious if it's challenged.
      • Breakdown: You feel your belief is under attack and will defend it fiercely.
    • 15. CATATONIC: You withdraw inward to cope.
      • Under Distress: You find it hard to speak and may shut down.
      • Breakdown: You halt actions, adopt a neutral posture, and become nearly mute, passively resisting interaction.
    • 16. BURDENED: You carry deeper troubles than most.
      • Under Distress: Your underlying issues surface, affecting your behavior.
      • Breakdown: Combine behaviors from two traits, intensifying your reactions.
    • 17. COMPULSIVE LIAR: You habitually lie, even without cause.
      • Under Distress: Your lies become elaborate and hard to maintain.
      • Breakdown: You create a false reality and insist others accept it.
    • 18. MANIPULATIVE: You influence others for personal gain.
      • Under Distress: You resort to deceit and exploitation.
      • Breakdown: You may manipulate allies into dangerous situations for your benefit.
    • 19. OBSESSIVE: You fixate on thoughts or ideas.
      • Under Distress: Obsessions dominate your actions.
      • Breakdown: You can't focus on anything else until satisfying your obsession.
    • 20. PESSIMISTIC: You expect the worst outcomes.
      • Under Distress: Your negativity affects group morale.
      • Breakdown: You're immobilized by hopelessness.
    • 21. RECKLESS: You take unnecessary risks.
      • Under Distress: You disregard safety measures.
      • Breakdown: You engage in dangerous actions without considering consequences.
    • 22. JEALOUS: You envy others' successes or possessions.
      • Under Distress: You undermine or sabotage others.
      • Breakdown: You attempt to take what others have by any means.
    • 23. DECEITFUL: Honesty isn't your policy.
      • Under Distress: You lie to protect yourself or gain an advantage.
      • Breakdown: You can't tell the truth, even when necessary.
    • 24. ARROGANT: You believe you're superior.
      • Under Distress: You belittle others and ignore advice.
      • Breakdown: Overconfidence leads you to make poor decisions.
    • 25. CONTROLLING: You need to be in charge.
      • Under Distress: You become domineering.
      • Breakdown: You attempt to control every aspect, causing conflict.
    • 26. STUBBORN: You refuse to change your mind.
      • Under Distress: You reject others' ideas.
      • Breakdown: You insist on your way, regardless of consequences.
    • 27. IMPULSIVE: You act without thinking.
      • Under Distress: You make hasty decisions.
      • Breakdown: You take immediate action without consideration.
    • 28. ENVIOUS: You desire what others have.
      • Under Distress: You fixate on others' possessions or status.
      • Breakdown: You might steal or sabotage to level the field.
    • 29. APATHETIC: You lack interest or concern.
      • Under Distress: You disengage from situations.
      • Breakdown: You become unresponsive to events around you.
    • 30. NARCISSISTIC: You have an inflated sense of self-importance.
      • Under Distress: You demand special treatment.
      • Breakdown: You believe only you can solve problems, dismissing others.
    • 31. MASOCHISTIC: You find satisfaction in your own discomfort.
      • Under Distress: You may self-sabotage or take unnecessary risks.
      • Breakdown: You engage in self-destructive behaviors.
    • 32. UNTRUSTING: You doubt others' intentions.
      • Under Distress: You suspect betrayal from those around you.
      • Breakdown: You isolate yourself, refusing help.
    • 33. GLUTTONOUS: You overindulge in pleasures.
      • Under Distress: You seek immediate gratification, neglecting duties.
      • Breakdown: You binge excessively, ignoring consequences.
    • 34. IRRATIONAL: You abandon logic and reason.
      • Under Distress: You make illogical decisions.
      • Breakdown: You cannot be reasoned with, acting unpredictably.
    • 35. VINDICTIVE: You hold grudges and seek revenge.
      • Under Distress: You dwell on past slights.
      • Breakdown: You focus solely on settling scores, regardless of cost.
    • 36. FRACTIOUS: You're irritable and prone to conflict.
      • Under Distress: You pick fights over minor issues.
      • Breakdown: You become uncooperative and disruptive.
    • 37. OBSTINATE: You're unyielding and inflexible.
      • Under Distress: You resist any change or compromise.
      • Breakdown: You become a barrier to progress and group harmony.
    • 38. SUPERSTITIOUS: You believe in omens and rituals.
      • Under Distress: You rely on superstitions to make decisions.
      • Breakdown: You refuse to act without a 'sign' or ritual.
    • 39. WORKAHOLIC: You're addicted to work.
      • Under Distress: You immerse yourself in tasks to avoid issues.
      • Breakdown: You neglect health and relationships, focusing only on work.
    • 40. HYPOCHONDRIAC: You fear illness excessively.
      • Under Distress: You misinterpret symptoms as serious ailments.
      • Breakdown: You believe you're critically ill, hindering your actions.
    • 41. RESENTFUL: You hold onto past wrongs.
      • Under Distress: You bring up old grievances.
      • Breakdown: You act out against perceived offenders, even if unwarranted.
    • 42. INDECISIVE: You struggle to make choices.
      • Under Distress: You're paralyzed by options.
      • Breakdown: You're incapable of deciding without external intervention.
    • 43. GUILT-RIDDEN: You're burdened by remorse.
      • Under Distress: You fixate on past mistakes.
      • Breakdown: You may confess to wrongs, real or imagined, distracting from current issues.
    • 44. SCHADENFREUDE: You take pleasure in others' misfortunes.
      • Under Distress: You may cause or amplify others' failures.
      • Breakdown: You actively create situations where others will fail.
    • 45. COMPULSIVE CLEANER: You need cleanliness and order.
      • Under Distress: You focus on tidiness over urgent matters.
      • Breakdown: You stop everything to clean compulsively, ignoring pressing issues.
    • 46. ESCAPIST: You avoid reality through distractions.
      • Under Distress: You retreat into fantasy or indulgent activities.
      • Breakdown: You detach completely from reality, becoming unresponsive.
    • 47. CONTROL FREAK: You micromanage every detail.
      • Under Distress: You can't delegate tasks, overwhelming yourself.
      • Breakdown: You try to handle everything alone, leading to burnout.
    • 48. PROCRASTINATOR: You delay tasks habitually.
      • Under Distress: You avoid responsibilities, causing delays.
      • Breakdown: You refuse to act, even when action is critical.
    • 49. PERFECTIONIST: Nothing meets your standards.
      • Under Distress: You obsess over minor flaws.
      • Breakdown: You're immobilized by the impossibility of perfection.
    • 50. EMOTIONLESS: You suppress your feelings.
      • Under Distress: You become detached and indifferent.
      • Breakdown: You can't empathize, making cold, calculated decisions without regard for others.
