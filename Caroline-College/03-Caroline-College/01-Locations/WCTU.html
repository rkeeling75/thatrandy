    Caroline College

    Caroline College

    Overview

    WCTU is the student-run radio station of Caroline College, broadcasting on AM radio. Its slogan, "The Heartbeat of Watashuka," highlights its connection to the campus and the surrounding community. WCTU features a wide range of shows, from music to talk radio, often showcasing indie bands and student discussions. The station is a popular place for college students to unwind, either by tuning in or by participating as DJs.

    • Location: Housed in the basement of the McElhenney Journalism School, the WCTU studio is small, cluttered, and packed with old records, tapes, and broadcasting equipment. The walls are lined with posters of bands and events, creating a vibrant yet somewhat chaotic atmosphere. The station operates 24/7, with students rotating shifts.

    • Popular Programs:

      • "Night Vibes": A late-night show hosted by Michelina Plum that plays classical and ambient music, providing a calm, relaxing soundtrack for late-night studying or sleep.
      • "News and Roots": A morning show hosted by Albert Hawkins, blending local news, weather, and folk music. It's a favorite for students and early risers who prefer a mellow start to their day.
      • "College Chaos": A high-energy, student-hosted talk show featuring the latest in college gossip, pranks, and music. It’s often the go-to for Caroline College students looking to blow off steam.

    WCTU Radio Station Weekly Program Schedule (Fall 1983)

    5:00 AM – 9:00 AM: "News and Roots" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Albert Hawkins
    • Description: A morning blend of local news, weather, and Americana/folk music to kick off the day. Albert keeps students and locals informed on current events in both Watashuka and the Caroline College campus.

    9:00 AM – 9:30 AM: "Morning Report" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Various (Student Contributors)
    • Description: A quick local news segment that covers campus life, community updates, and interviews with key figures from the college and the town.

    9:30 AM – 12:00 PM: "Lunch Break Jams" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Julia Richards (Replaced in March 1983 after Sam Lang’s disappearance)
    • Description: Popular music and song requests to brighten up midday. College students and staff often phone in with quirky stories and requests for shout-outs.

    12:00 PM – 12:30 PM: "Midday Report" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Various (Student Contributors)
    • Description: Midday news featuring sports, events, and some talk of local politics. A casual but informative show for those needing a brief update during lunch.

    12:30 PM – 2:00 PM: "The Study Hour" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Carl Yates
    • Description: Classical music and instrumental tracks meant to help students focus on their studies. Quiet, thoughtful commentary with minimal interruptions.

    2:00 PM – 4:00 PM: "College Chaos" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Rotating student hosts (Current: Matt and Liz)
    • Description: A high-energy, fun show focusing on gossip, college life, and pop culture. Call-ins from students make this show an unpredictable favorite.

    4:00 PM – 4:30 PM: "Evening Report" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Various (Student Contributors)
    • Description: A late-afternoon news update covering the day's headlines, local happenings, and student events. Quick interviews with students and staff cap off the day.

    4:30 PM – 6:00 PM: "Golden Oldies" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Jack Carter (Replaced Ron Sterling's)
    • Description: A nostalgic trip through classic rock, jazz, and '50s/'60s hits. Jack shares fun facts and stories behind the music.

    6:00 PM – 8:00 PM: "The Alternative" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Annie Tran
    • Description: Alternative, underground music that introduces students to up-and-coming bands and unique tracks not heard on mainstream radio.

    10:00 PM – 5:00 AM: "Night Vibes" (Mon-Fri)

    • Host: Michelina Plum
    • Description: A late-night show of classical, ambient music to relax or study by. Michelina’s soft voice and ethereal playlists help late-night listeners unwind.

    Saturday and Sunday Weekend Programming

    5:00 AM – 7:00 AM: "Watashuka Lutheran Hour" (Sundays Only)

    • Host: Pastor Daniel Mueller
    • Description: A weekly sermon, religious music, and reflections from Pastor Mueller, the head of All Saints Church in Watashuka. He delivers community prayers and thoughtful messages.

    7:00 AM – 9:00 AM: "Reflections of Faith" (Sundays Only)

    • Host: Father Jeremy Rotten (from Caroline College’s St. John’s Chapel)
    • Description: Discussions on faith, philosophy, and classical religious texts. Father Rotten engages with a more academic audience, blending religious reflection with college life.

    9:00 AM – 11:00 AM: "Soundtrack Saturdays/Sundays" (Sat-Sun)

    • Host: Linda Myers (Replaced Mark Philips)
    • Description: Focused on scores from movies, television, and video games, Linda explores the historical and musical significance of iconic soundtracks.

    11:00 AM – 1:00 PM: "Community Voices" (Saturdays Only)

    • Host: Various Local Guests
    • Description: A community program where Watashuka locals discuss their interests, from town issues to local hobbies. Mothers Opposing Metal often make appearances, passionately decrying the influence of heavy metal on the town's youth.

    1:00 PM – 3:00 PM: "Live from Campus" (Saturdays Only)

    • Host: Various Campus Musicians
    • Description: Live performances by Caroline College students, showcasing the diversity of talent on campus. Students submit their music or perform live for listeners.

    3:00 PM – 5:00 PM: "The Vinyl Vault" (Sat-Sun)

    • Host: Fred Hollister
    • Description: Rare and forgotten records, covering everything from old jazz and blues to 1960s folk music. Fred's deep knowledge of music history makes this a hit with collectors.

    5:00 PM – 7:00 PM: "Mystery Hour" (Sat-Sun)

    • Host: Greg Tanner (Replaced in Melissa Young)
    • Description: Radio drama featuring serialized detective stories and thrillers. Sometimes features scripts written by students or adaptations of classic mysteries.

    7:00 PM – 9:00 PM: "Midnight Musings" (Sundays Only)

    • Host: Alice Graves
    • Description: Poetry readings, quiet jazz, and philosophical discussions to end the week on a reflective note.

    9:00 PM – 12:00 AM: "Underground Echoes" (Saturdays Only)

    • Host: David Scott (Replaced Amy Lewis)
    • Description: A showcase of obscure, underground tracks from local and international scenes. This show is beloved by students who enjoy experimental and offbeat music.h-energy talk show focusing on college life, hosted by a rotating cast of students. The show mixes music, gossip, and humor, making it one of the most entertaining programs.
