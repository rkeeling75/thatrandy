    Mike and Ruth Garrison

    Mike and Ruth Garrison

    Mike and Ruth Garrison

    It’s another sunny afternoon at the Frozen Cow Burger Stand. The line stretches down the sidewalk as locals and students eagerly await their favorite meals. Mike stands at the counter, laughing with customers and cracking jokes, his long-ago hippie roots showing in his relaxed demeanor. Ruth, ever the efficient one, works the grill with practiced ease. She glances at Mike, shaking her head with a fond smile. Despite all the hustle, the two seem perfectly in sync, their connection a comforting fixture in the town of Watashuka.

    Mike and Ruth Garrison are the proud owners of the Frozen Cow Burger Stand, a beloved 1950s-style diner in Watashuka. Mike, a former hippie who drifted into town in the late 60s, fell in love with Ruth when he stopped at a burger stand where she worked. Mike was lost and in need of a meal. When taking his order, Ruth asked Mike what he was doing in town. Mike said, under the influence, that he was on his way to Alaska. "Why would you want to go way up there?" she asked politely. “I'm heading up north to check out a free commune.” Ruth misheard him over the noise of the diner - thinking he said he was "heading up north to check out a frozen cow." This was the funniest thing she ever heard and busted out laughing. Mike thought the sound of her laughter was the most beautiful thing he ever heard.

    From that moment on, Mike worked hard to buy Ruth her own burger stand. That misunderstanding has become a staple in the community for decades now. And while he gave the drugs up a long time ago, his memory never fully recovered. He readily admits that Ruth is the brains of the outfit.

    Mike Garrison is in his early 60s, with a stocky build and a weathered, easygoing face that often carries a big, friendly smile. His thick, silver-gray hair still hints at the carefree days of his youth, and he usually sports a well-worn ball cap or the classic paper diner hat while working at the stand. Mike's style is a blend of practical and nostalgic, often seen wearing a white short-sleeved button-up shirt, tucked into his faded jeans or diner uniform. His voice is warm and booming, quick to share a joke or story from his hippie days, though his past drug use often leads to some comically hazy recollections.

    Ruth Garrison is in her late 50s, shorter and more slender than Mike, with neatly styled salt-and-pepper hair often tied back in a simple bun. Her face has a warm yet practical expression, and while she doesn’t smile as often as Mike, when she does, it’s with a quiet, knowing grace. Ruth’s sharp blue eyes seem to notice everything around her, including the details Mike often misses. Her attire is always neat and functional, typically a white blouse beneath a striped apron, making her look like the efficient, hardworking heart of the Frozen Cow. While Ruth is more reserved, she’s also quick-witted and known for her dry sense of humor, especially when it comes to teasing Mike.

    Together, they’re a contrasting but complementary pair—Mike, the talkative charmer, and Ruth, the quiet but sharp backbone of the operation.
