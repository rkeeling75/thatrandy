    The Metalheads (Local Counterculture and Occult Fighters)

    Overview:

    In the early 1980s, Watashuka’s Metalhead community is a tight-knit group of teenagers and young adults who rally around heavy metal music, tabletop role-playing games, and the rebellion of youth counterculture. What makes them unique in Watashuka is their hidden role as one of the few groups actively fighting against the occult forces that threaten the town. Unlike the stereotypes of the time, which painted them as devil-worshipping delinquents, these outcasts use their knowledge of obscure lore, occult symbols, and music to resist eldritch forces, often working in the shadows to prevent horrors that most townspeople never see.

    Role in Town:

    Viewed with suspicion and disdain by the more conservative residents of Watashuka, the Metalheads are frequently blamed for vandalism, strange disappearances, and even weather anomalies. Their loud music, punk rock attire, and unconventional interests make them easy targets for the Mothers Opposing Metal group, the town’s moral watchdogs. However, behind their rebellious exterior, they are actually one of the few factions in Watashuka actively working to combat the cults and dark powers manipulating the town.

    Base of Operations:

    Many of the Metalheads hang out at Jack’s Surfer: Comics & Games or at the Blue Heron Pub. This group is heavily influenced by metal music’s cryptic lyrics and the imagery of fantasy role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons, which have inadvertently led them to discover real occult knowledge hidden within the town's dark history.

    Notable Figures:

    • “Raven” Becky Thompson: One of the few female Metalheads in town, Becky is fiercely independent and well-versed in occult symbols and esoteric rituals. She spends her days researching local folklore at The Prince: New & Used Books and her nights planning counter-attacks against the cultists, using her knowledge of heavy metal and mythology.
    • Eddie “Styx” Delano: Eddie is the town’s go-to guy for rituals that require certain "artifacts" like skulls, daggers, and ritual books. He’s resourceful, knowing how to procure these items without raising suspicion. Despite his rough appearance, Eddie is serious about fighting the occult forces, and he has a strong moral code, believing they must protect the innocent from the eldritch horrors lurking in the town.

    Activities:

    • Occult Research: The Metalheads spend much of their time researching occult symbols and rituals that can be found in their music and games, giving them an edge when it comes to identifying cult activity.
    • Disrupting Rituals: They've made it their mission to disrupt local cults by graffitiing occult symbols that interfere with rituals, performing counter-rituals themselves, and even engaging in direct action when necessary.
    • Underground Communication: Using Dave's radio show, the Metalheads pass coded messages to each other about cult movements and activities.
    • Public Perception: Feared and misunderstood by the general populace, the Metalheads are often accused of satanic rituals themselves, especially by the Mothers Opposing Metal (MOM) group. Their music and games are seen as gateways to evil, even though the reality is that they are often the ones standing between the town and much darker forces.
