    My Dear Robert,

    I hope you are enjoying your extended sabbatical. I look forward to your return and our afternoon "walking meetings". It is a shame that you were scrubbed from the Challenger mission. I am sure you will be given another chance at the shuttle.

    Back home we are getting ready for the new semester. As I sit here, the clock on my desk ticking away the seconds, I can't help but feel a sense of anticipation about the upcoming academic year. I'm sure this year's class will not suffer the same fate as those of years past.

    I know I mention this often, but I believe there is something evil casting a shadow over our school. But since the summer, with each passing day, I become more convinced that this year will be different. I feel that big changes are coming for Watashuka and Caroline College.

    ...at the very least, I hope these freshmen fare better than past classes.

    Last year's group seems to have suffered more than most. As of last report, we've lost over half of the class of '82 by withdrawal... or worse. If it weren't for the grants, I don't think the school would be able to continue.

    While we are on sad topics, I promised you a follow up about the Martha Johnson incident. Sgt. Ducochick still has made no arrests but he did call me this morning. He said he needed to speak with me at once. Even though he pressed, I told him I simply couldn't put off attending to my work until after supper. The good detective stressed how important it was. But the students come first!
