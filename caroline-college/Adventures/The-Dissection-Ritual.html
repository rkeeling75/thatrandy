    The Dissection Ritual

    Sep 29, 20249 min read

    Adventure: The Dissection Ritual

    Overview

    The players are introduced to a mysterious ritual disguised as a mundane lab exercise. Church of the Delayed Blossom is trying to complete a summoning ritual under the guise of a biology class at Caroline College. The disappearance of Amy Mann, an undercover government agent posing as a valley girl student, serves as a red herring, distracting the players from the true threat: the cult's ongoing ritual. The adventure spans a week, with moments of investigation, spooky phenomena, and a final confrontation.

    Scene 1: The First Night - The Interrupted Ritual

    • When: August 29th, 1983
    • Location: Caroline College, Biology Lab (Night)
    • Important Characters:
      • Substitute (cultist of Shub-Niggurath): Leading the ritual disguised as a mundane lab exercise.
      • Students (including Amy Mann): Distracted and unaware of what’s happening.
    • Purpose: Introduce the ritual and hint at something supernatural, but interrupt it before it fully manifests.
    • Threat/Puzzle: Players unknowingly arrange lab tools in a sigil. Eerie events follow: flickering lights, whispers, and a sudden, chilling wind. Everyone panics and flees the lab.
    • Spooky Interruption: A minor supernatural manifestation—spectral figure, cold gusts, and doors slamming—forces the evacuation of the lab.
    • Clue: A torn piece of paper with sigil symbols is left behind.

    Scene 2: Day 2 - The Disappearance of Amy Mann

    • Location: Campus (Lab, Dorms, Library, etc.)
    • Important Characters:
      • Amy Mann: Disappears the morning after the lab.
      • Fellow Students: Worried about Amy’s disappearance.
      • Campus Police and Government Agents (undercover): Involved in the investigation, with agents pretending to be ordinary police.
    • Purpose: Shift focus to Amy’s disappearance, introducing a red herring and giving the players reason to investigate.
    • Threat/Puzzle: Amy doesn’t show up for class. Players may join the search for her, encountering undercover agents trying to keep them away from the truth.
    • Clue: A journal in Amy’s dorm room reveals cryptic notes, surveillance logs, and advanced science research.

    Scene 3: Day 3 - The Search Intensifies

    • Location: Across Watashuka (Library, Amy’s Dorm, local park)
    • Important Characters:
      • Amy’s Friends/Acquaintances: Offering misleading or vague information.
      • Government Agents: Disguised as helpful police, subtly steering players away from sensitive areas.
    • Purpose: Deepen the mystery with red herrings and mislead the players into thinking Amy’s disappearance had an Earthly cause.
    • Threat/Puzzle: Players may find shoe prints leading to a wooded area or Amy’s notebook in the library. Red herrings include sightings of suspicious men or Amy’s scarf at the lake.
    • Subtle Clue: A missing student ID and a forged one hint at Amy’s true identity.

    Scene 4: Day 4-5 - Unsettling Discoveries

    • Location: Biology Lab, Amy’s Dorm, Campus Library
    • Important Characters:
      • Substitute (cultist): More agitated, as they must complete the ritual before the week ends.
      • Students/Investigators: Still searching for Amy.
    • Purpose: Build tension as players investigate, noticing that the ritual may still be ongoing.
    • Threat/Puzzle: The lab shows residual effects of the ritual—symbols burned into tables, strange shadows, and odd sensations. Subtle red herrings lead players away from the ritual.
    • Subtle Clue: The substitute wears occult symbols as jewelry, hinting at their involvement.

    Scene 5: Day 6-7 - The Ritual Resumes

    • Location: Biology Lab (Night)
    • Important Characters:
      • Substitute (cultist): Desperately trying to complete the ritual.
      • Other Students: Still distracted by Amy’s disappearance.
    • Purpose: Final confrontation where the players can stop or fail to stop the ritual.
    • Threat/Puzzle: The substitute resumes the ritual, with phenomena such as levitating objects, flickering lights, and unnatural cold. Players must decide to confront the substitute or disrupt the sigil.
    • Subtle Clue: Amy’s journal hints at the cult’s activities, adding more layers to the mystery of her disappearance.
    • Climax: Players confront the substitute and try to stop the ritual before the entity fully manifests.

    Possible Outcomes

    1. The Cult Succeeds in Completing the Ritual

    • Effect: A lesser entity of Shub-Niggurath manifests briefly, warping reality and causing mental or physical damage to the players.
    • Consequences:
      • Physical Damage: Players may suffer scars or mysterious injuries.
      • Mental Trauma: Permanent Sanity loss and mental disorders (phobias, paranoia, hallucinations).
      • New Motivation: The players are driven to stop the cult from summoning something worse.
    • Hook for Future Adventures: Lingering effects—odd phenomena, people acting strangely, warped objects—hint that the entity’s influence remains.

    2. The Players Stop the Ritual

    • Effect: The ritual is disrupted before it fully manifests, but reality warps temporarily, causing hallucinations and Sanity loss.
    • Consequences:
      • Physical Evidence: Ritual scars and residual energy in the environment.
      • Mental Consequences: Players suffer from flashbacks or irrational fears related to the event.
      • New Motivation: The players realize they’ve only delayed the cult, motivating them to investigate further.
    • Hook for Future Adventures: Clues about the cult’s broader network and future plans emerge.

    Follow-Up Adventures

    1. The Vanishing Farm

    • Premise: A local Amish farm is mysteriously abandoned, with all inhabitants missing and livestock mutilated.
    • Connection: The symbols found on the farm match the sigils from the biology lab ritual.
    • New Motivation: The players investigate the Amish’s secret role in protecting the town from supernatural forces.

    2. The Haunting of Caroline College

    • Premise: After the biology lab incident, students report hauntings and eerie phenomena on campus.
    • Connection: Residual energy from the failed or successful ritual causes hauntings, leading to ghostly manifestations of past cultists.
    • New Motivation: Players must banish lingering spirits and uncover the cult’s ties to the college.

    3. The Forgotten Burial Grounds

    • Premise: Sacred Ho-Chunk burial grounds are disturbed, angering spirits and causing animals to act violently.
    • Connection: The cult is using the burial grounds for another ritual. The players discover ancient ties between the cult and the Ho-Chunk tribe.
    • New Motivation: Players work with the Ho-Chunk tribe to stop the cult from raising something from the burial grounds.

    4. The Disappearing Agents

    • Premise: Law enforcement and government officials investigating Project Caroline disappear one by one.
    • Connection: The players realize that Project Caroline’s own agents are being targeted by either the cult or internal factions.
    • New Motivation: The players investigate the agents’ disappearances and uncover hidden files about Project Caroline’s secret projects.

    5. The Returning Entity

    • Premise: Strange weather patterns and eerie phenomena suggest that something summoned by the ritual is trying to return.
    • Connection: The entity from the biology lab ritual is slowly breaking through into reality, causing odd occurrences and sightings of a mysterious figure.
    • New Motivation: The players must find a way to seal the breach between dimensions and prevent the entity from fully manifesting.

    Conclusion

    This adventure sets the stage for a broader investigation into the cult of Shub-Niggurath and the hidden activities of Project Caroline. The players’ choices in this first adventure will shape the direction of the campaign, whether they are driven by their encounter with the supernatural or the mystery of Amy Mann’s disappearance. The follow-up adventures provide a variety of ways to expand the world, introducing new dangers and deeper connections to the mythos of Watashuka.

    Amy Mann "Valley Girl" Dialogue Suggestions

    1. Loudly Bragging About Non-Existent Parties

    • “Oh my God, you guys, like, last weekend? I was so wasted, I, like, didn’t even know where I was! It was, like, totally insane. We, like, drank sooo much Bartles & Jaymes. Have you ever had Bartles & Jaymes? It’s, like, so fancy.”
    • “Yeah, so, I met this guy—he’s so cute—and, like, we went to this totally secret rave. He said he’s, like, in pre-law? Ugh, so hot. But I totally don’t even, like, remember his name.”

    2. Talking About Academic Topics in a Weird, Inappropriate Way (Slipping Up)

    • “Like, have you guys ever wondered, like, if you could, like, totally stop time? I mean, think about it, like, if you accelerated, like, particles to near-light speed, you’d totally experience time dilation. Like, so crazy, right?”
    • “OMG, bodies are, like, so fascinating, you know? Like, all those little mitochondria doing their thing, generating ATP, like, it’s just so cute. It’s like they’re partying in your cells! Totally radical!”
    • “You ever think, like, the universe could be, like, a simulation? ‘Cause, like, quantum fluctuations? Totally feels like it, right? Whatever.”

    3. Overcompensating to Fit In

    • “So, like, when are we gonna get to the part of the class where we get to cut stuff open? Like, ew, but also, like, sooo cool, you know?”
    • “I’m totally not into, like, math or science or whatever, but, like, my dad said I had to take this class. He’s, like, a really smart guy, though, so I guess he’s right. Whatevs, I’ll figure it out.”
    • “Ugh, dissecting things is, like, sooo grody, but also kinda hot, you know? Like, I’m totally not into it, but, like, I could get used to it.”

    4. Loud and Inappropriate Comments

    • “Like, I totally blacked out the other night. We were playing, like, beer pong, and I think I, like, invented a new math formula or something? Hah, that’d be, like, so funny, right?”
    • “Oh my God, is this where we, like, cut open frogs or whatever? I heard my friend in bio say they squirt when you poke ‘em. Gag me, right?”
    • “This guy, like, came up to me and was all, like, serious? He was, like, ‘You should study more,’ and I’m like, whatever, dude, like, I don’t have time for that, you know? I’m here to, like, have fun!”

    5. Accidentally Too Smart

    • “So, like, when the mitochondria produce ATP during oxidative phosphorylation, it’s, like, sooo efficient, right? I mean, it’s kinda boring, but also, like, fascinating in a molecular biology sense, you know?”
    • “Yeah, well, when I was, like, totally wasted the other night, I started thinking about how Heisenberg's uncertainty principle basically implies we can never know the true state of a particle’s velocity and position at the same time. Ugh, like, what a buzzkill, right?”
    • "Wait, don’t you guys know that the Pythagorean theorem only works in Euclidean geometry? Like, duh! But, whatever, right?”
