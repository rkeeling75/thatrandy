    Zones

    Custom Rules for Zones in the Campaign Setting

    Ah, the Zones. They are what remains of a world fractured and scarred, places with their own set of rules, and each one a harsh master. Let me tell you, surviving in a Zone that isn't your own? Thatâs like trying to breathe underwater. You might manage it for a while, but the longer you stay, the harder it gets. Hereâs how it works.

    Zone Dependency

    Every species has a Zone that they call home, a place that fits them like a worn pair of boots. Humans have the Sheltered Route, halflings their Northern Fields, goblins their Echoing Hills, and Gravlingsâwell, the Gravling Zone is a place of the living dead. Leaving your home Zone isnât impossible, but it comes at a steep cost. Each Zone affects outsiders differently, and without the right precautions, you'll soon find that even the air you breathe or the food you eat can become your enemy.

    (A character must make a Constitution Saving Throw, increasing in difficulty based on the time spent outside their home Zone, or suffer exhaustion. This exhaustion cannot be recovered through rest! Recovery of a point of zone induced exhaustion requires returning home and spending uninterrupted time there equal to the time spent away multiplied by the furthest number of zones removed from home the character reached. Even if they spent one minute in a zone 4 steps removed, their multiplier is 4 . Each level of exhaustion must be recovered individually, and any time spent outside the home Zone restarts the time required to recover that exhaustion point. Once that time has elapsed, that point of exhaustion can be recovered as normal. After a point of zone induced exhaustion is recovered, the process starts all over for the next one.)

    (The further you travel from your home Zone, the harsher the penalties become and the more frequent the checks are made. Characters must make a Constitution Saving Throw of DC 12+ furthest number of zones visited from home: one Zone away requires weekly checks at DC 13, two Zones away requires daily checks at DC 14, three Zones away requires hourly checks at DC 15, and four Zones away, or more, requires checks with every action taken at the appropriate DC. This DC stays at its maximum level until all points of zone of induced exhaustion are recovered. However, checks are no long made once the character returns to their home zone unless they leave that zone before all zone induced exhaustion points are recovered. )

    (Each point of Zone-induced exhaustion also causes damage that cannot be healed until all exhaustion is recovered, d4 HP, and the effects grow more severe as exhaustion levels increase.)

    Example Zone Effects on Species

    • Humans: Outside the Sheltered Route, humans begin to weaken. Healing becomes difficult, and wounds fester easily. (Necrotic damage, healing benefits are halved after the first point of exhaustion, long rests provide no healing after the second, and short rests provide no healing after the third.)
    • Halflings: Halflings thrive on the bounty of the Northern Fields. Outside, they cannot gain proper sustenance. They need food grown in the Fields; anything else only delays their starvation. (Physical damage, postponement of exhaustion checks if consuming two full meals of homegrown food daily, up to three Zones away. Without at least one meal of native food a day, they suffer an extra level of exhaustion whenever one is take; zone induced or otherwise.)
    • Goblins: The Echoing Hills have shaped goblins into creatures of heightened senses. Away from the Warrens, their senses betray them. (Necrotic damage. The first point of zone induced exhaustion gives disadvantage on perception (hearing) checks, zone induced damage is multiplied by exhaustion level, and all rolls are made with an additional -1 times exhaustion level penalty on top of those imposed by normal exhaustion rules.)
    • Gravlings: Gravlings outside their Zone lose hold of their second life. (Physical damage, each point of exhaustion results in a limb falling off, never to be restored!)

    Potions and Zones

    Traveling beyond one's Zone isnât always a death sentence, but it requires preparation and understanding. Some brewers can craft Zone Adaption Potions to stave off the ill effects of a foreign Zone for a limited time. Their effectiveness decreases the further one travels from home.

    • Zone Adaption Potions: Potions function with reduced effectiveness the further one travels from home. These are expensive potions to make, both in time and cost, and they are specific to each species.

      • Basic Potions: Provide protection for 24 hours when one Zone away. No effect beyond that.
      • Greater Potions: Provide 7 days of protection when one Zone away, and 24 hours when two Zones away. No effect beyond two Zones.
      • Superior Potions: Provide one month of protection when one Zone away, one week when two Zones away, and 1 day when three Zones away. No effect beyond three Zones.
      • Major Potions: Could potentially offer extended protection, even up to four Zones away, though such potions are exceedingly rare and require truly unique components.

    Inhabitants and Zone Synergy

    There are benefits to being within one's Zone too. Each species draws strength from their home, a sense of synergy that canât be replicated elsewhere.

    • Humans in the Sheltered Route: They recover from ailments more quickly. (Advantage on saving throws against diseases, recover exhaustion twice as fast; including zone induced exhaustion.)
    • Halflings in the Northern Fields: They heal more efficiently and require less sustenance. (Regain additional hit points (2x) when using Hit Dice and need half as much food.)
    • Goblins in the Echoing Warrens: Their senses are sharpened, particularly their hearing. (Advantage on perception checks involving hearing.)
    • Gravlings in the Gravling Zone: They do not decay and slowly regain vitality. (Regain hit points each hour spent in their home Zone; may spend a hit die per hour without taking a short rest.)

    Shifting Zones and the Unpredictable Boundaries

    The world isnât static, and neither are the Zones. Boundaries can shift unpredictably, which can be a blessing or a curse depending on where you stand. These shifts are known as Zonal Fluxes, and they can change a fertile field into a desolate wasteland overnight.

    • Zonal Flux Events: These occur at random intervals, often without warning. When a Zone Flux happens, all creatures within the boundary must act quickly to avoid being caught in the abrupt change. (Dex Saving Throwing DC 12, for all characters within 1 mile of the border; how lucky are you?).Â 
    • Effects of Being Caught in a Flux: Those caught within the shifting boundaries may experience the negative effects of both Zones simultaneously until they transition fully into the new Zone. This often results in confusion, disorientation, and extreme fatigue. (Disadvantage on all rolls for the next 24 hours.)

It isn't nice or fair, but itâs what the old civilization left us. Step across at your peril, but if you must, do so with open eyes and a full understanding of what youâre risking. Preparation, caution, and a little bit of luck might see you through. But if you think for a moment that youâre invincible, the Zones will be all too happy to prove otherwise.
